What OINP is

OINP is the company-wide platform every Intuit product team (QuickBooks, TurboTax, Credit Karma, Mailchimp, ProSeries, Lacerte, plus several internal product surfaces) uses to deliver transactional and marketing notifications across Email, SMS, RCS, Voice, Push, Tray, In-App, and ICE. The platform spans authoring (RFM), delivery (channel adapters), event ingestion (Pulsar), and the management surfaces (search, dashboards, promotion).

I own four domains on OINP end-to-end:

  • Authoring experience — the modernised RFM portal (schema, APIs, search, preview, promotion to prod), the parity-validation layer against the legacy stack, and the onboarding documentation partner teams consume.
  • RCS delivery — vendor integration, schema and API design, two-way inbound, cross-swim-lane Pulsar routing to consumer teams, consent, SMS fallback.
  • SMS delivery — the SMS launch on the modernised stack, Critical Money Alerts SMS integration, multi-subaccount Twilio support, consent flows.
  • Notification Tray — the in-product inbox across Intuit surfaces. Reliability forensics, the proposal to migrate off Cassandra+Solr, in-app incident leadership.

Beyond the four domains, I act as a cross-org integration surface — partner teams across 10+ products who can't tell whether a missing notification is upstream, OINP, or vendor land with me first to disambiguate. Operationally I prepare the services I own for tax peak — load testing, pre-scaling, runbooks — and stay on through the night shifts the season requires; both TY24 and TY25 closed with my services holding zero 5xx through the window.

Directional decisions across the work

  • Migrate, don't patch, when patch budget exceeds migration budget. Made this call publicly to the customer-comms team for the Notification Tray (40 GB Cassandra+Solr → managed DB), drove it for the legacy authoring stack (RFM Revamp), and pushed for elimination of the legacy scheduling service before TY25. Each removed a chronic failure source.
  • Parity over hope. Stood up comms-comparator as the parity backbone for the entire RFM migration. Ran a 7-day prod audit: 2.9M reads, 99.987% parity, every failing class root-caused. Without it, we could not have moved 100% of traffic to the new stack before tax peak.
  • Phased rollout to avoid throwaway work. S3 thumbnails → Elasticsearch → domain-event resiliency, each phase usable in its own right. Default approach across the team now for "add capability X to product Y."
  • API contract before implementation. Pushed this with every junior engineer touching a new surface. Pushed back in the platform leaders forum against a peer's BFF proposal — used notif-common's history of scope-creep as the precedent, and proposed gateway routing as the simpler primitive. The decision held; no BFF.
  • Escalate to leadership when peer-level chatops stalls. When the messaging-infra team wouldn't approve cross-swim-lane routing to prod (blocking two consumer-team integrations), escalated in the platform leaders forum for manager contacts, scheduled the cross-team meeting myself, negotiated a prod rollout timeline.
  • Demonstrate before proposing. Rather than write a doc for the home-page redesign, generated previews of 3,550 production notifications using the new Preview API and hosted the visual mockup. The proposal landed because the data was already there.
